Technical Projects

I come from a non-traditional technical background. I am a B.A. candidate in American Studies, and I intend to apply for the M.S. co-terminal program in Computer Science as well. I've been exposed to many technologies in the past. I am familiar with Java, especially in the context of Android development, as well as C++ and JavaScript. I have experience working with and figuring out various APIs as well, including Gmail and Google Drive technologies and various email marketing tools. In addition, I'm fairly well-versed in HTML and CSS — everything you see on this page right now was made from scratch in HTML5 and CSS because I truly enjoy the flexibility it allows.

I am largely self taught. I learned jQuery for a work project last summer: building the email marketing system for BrainMind, a neuroscience non-profit devoted to creating an ecosystem of academics, venture capitalists, and industry figures to champion responsible and conscientious neuro-innovation. We even managed to hit an impressive 40% CTR. A month later, I was finishing a class project, for a communication studies course, and realized I could make a cool game that taught some of the concepts in the class while teaching myself React — and Don't Be Awkward , a game about proxemics, was born.

I am currently developing a digitized version of CBT (cognitive behavioral therapy) thought records that I call Headway. It was a good way for me to learn Android development by building a simple database-backed app while creating someting with the potential to help some of the millions of Americans who suffer from a psychiatric illness. It started off purely as a passion project: I wanted to build something for my personal use based on the mental wellness tools that I had found most effective for me. CBT was well-structured and understandable, so it was a natural candidate for a technological tool. Within the project, I am implementing analytics to help users identify common emotional states and thoughts to foster emotional mindfulness and increase self-awareness of one's mental health, a novel method of enhancing traditional CBT via technology. Again, health is not the absence of disease — I truly believe any one can benefit from a little more emotional mindfulness here and there. Demoes will be provided upon request!

Writing

I'm a writer. I've always found it natural to communicate my thoughts in writing, whether I was working on a more serious article, writing stories, or composing poetry. I wanted to further that interest more in college, and so I joined the Stanford Daily, the university's student-run newspaper, in hopes of learning from my peers and improving my own craft. Due to my deep interest in electronic music and songwriting, I joined the music beat, where I was soon traveling to local concerts to cover large electronic music acts from Zedd to Porter Robinson.

I have some experience writing as well outside the world of music. I wrote a blog post for my fellowship program in Washington D.C. the summer after my freshman year about an experience we had meeting with a lobbyist for a major telecommunications company. In addition, as editor-in-chief of my high school newspaper, I covered a plethora of topics from Asian-American identity, personal narratives, and cultural satire.

Music

Music was certainly my first love. As a pianist for 15 years, I can't remember a time before I was sitting at the ivory keys. It was the first creative way that I discovered to express myself, and so even as a child, I grew to enjoy profoundly the timeless moments of music I spent at the piano. I briefly even considered a professional career in the field — but I was always searching for something new, and my interest turned to other things.

Music is still certainly a part of my life. Below you will find two electronic music tracks that I produced personally from scratch. In addition, I am currently a taking private jazz piano lessons at Stanford University on a scholarship.
